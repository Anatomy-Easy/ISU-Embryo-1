Q1. What is the definition of Embryology, according to the presentation?
Explanation: Embryology is defined as the study of prenatal development from fertilization to the fetal stage and birth.
Q2. The embryonic period, the most sensitive time for major congenital anomalies, covers which time frame?
Explanation: The embryonic period is designated as Weeks 3–8, which is the critical period for major congenital anomalies because organogenesis occurs during this time.
Q3. What is the key event that occurs during the Pre-Embryonic phase (Weeks 1-2)?
Explanation: The Pre-Embryonic phase (Weeks 1–2) includes fertilization, cleavage, and implantation.
Q4. Which developmental process establishes the three primary germ layers (Ectoderm, Mesoderm, Endoderm)?
Explanation: Gastrulation is the process that establishes the three primary germ layers (Ectoderm, Mesoderm, and Endoderm), which will give rise to all tissues and organs in the body.
Q5. Which of the following is NOT a major developmental signaling pathway mentioned in the presentation?
Explanation: The four critical developmental signaling pathways mentioned are HOX, SHH, WNT, and BMP.
Q6. The single cell formed by the union of a sperm and an oocyte during fertilization is called a/an:
Explanation: The Zygote is defined as the single cell formed by the union of a sperm and an oocyte during fertilization, occurring on Day 0–1.
Q7. Which layer of the trophoblast is the outer multinucleated layer that invades the uterine endometrium during implantation?
Explanation: During implantation (Week 2), the trophoblast differentiates into the inner Cytotrophoblast and the outer multinucleated Syncytiotrophoblast, which invades the endometrium.
Q8. Which key term describes the developing human organism during the period of major organogenesis (Week 3–8)?
Explanation: The Embryo is the developing human organism during the period of major organogenesis, spanning Week 3–8.
Q9. Neural Tube Defects (like spina bifida and anencephaly) result from a failure of neural tube closure during which weeks?
Explanation: Neural Tube Defects result from the failure of neural tube closure during Weeks 3–4.
Q10. From which primary germ layer does the epithelial lining of the digestive system and the parenchyma of the liver and pancreas primarily develop?
Explanation: The Endoderm is the inner layer that gives rise to the epithelial lining of the gastrointestinal tract and glandular organs like the liver and pancreas parenchyma.
Q11. What is the term for any agent or factor that can cause a birth defect or congenital anomaly during development?
Explanation: A Teratogen is defined as any agent or factor (drugs, alcohol, infections, radiation, etc.) that can cause a birth defect or congenital anomaly.
Q12. Which structure gives rise to the Central Nervous System (Brain and Spinal Cord) and the Retina?
Explanation: The Neuroectoderm (Neural Tube) gives rise to the CNS and Retina. The Peripheral Nervous System is mainly derived from Neural Crest.
Q13. Which tissue/organ is derived from the Lateral Plate Mesoderm?
Explanation: The Lateral Plate Mesoderm gives rise to the cardiovascular system, smooth muscle, and connective tissue of the limbs.
Q14. The process of rapid mitotic cell divisions of the zygote without growth, producing smaller cells called blastomeres, is known as:
Explanation: Cleavage is the rapid series of mitotic cell divisions of the zygote without growth, which produces smaller cells called blastomeres, occurring around Day 1–4.
Q15. What is the main clinical relevance of Embryology, according to the presentation?
Explanation: Embryology's clinical relevance includes explaining congenital malformations (birth defects), supporting imaging interpretation, and guiding reproductive medicine like IVF and genetic counseling.
